No, it's not just you. The Philippines is indeed getting hotter.

This heatmap shows the maximum heat index recorded in all Pagasa stations from April 15 to 30. A heat index combines temperature and relative humidity to measure the effects of excessive heat on people.

In those two weeks, Baguio City, north of the Philippines, recorded a heat index above 32 degrees Celsius, an unusually warm weather in the coldest part of the country.

Beyond Baguio, the situation was worse. Residents in majority of areas were either told to exercise "extreme caution" or warned of potential "danger" from extreme heat such as heat cramps of worse, a heat stroke.

A prevailing El Nino phenomenon since last year is contributing to the heat, but this year's scorching summer is unlikely to be an aberration. For the past decade ending 2023, temperatures in the country are rising year after year, data from the weather bureau show.

That, in turn, is making warm weather more common throughout the day.

It's more hot in the Philippines

Filipinos used to warm weather are finding this year's dry season tougher to bear

By Prinz Magtulis

May 3, 2024


Tropical climate in the Philippines makes Filipinos become used to warm weather all year round. But in normal cases, heat typically subsides by the evening. While that is still happening, the fall in temperature at night is no longer as pronounced as before. In fact, since 2014, minimum temperatures across several of Pagasa's weather stations are rising.
"Nighttime temperatures are increasing," said Gerry Bagtasa, professor at the Institute of Environmental Science and Meteorology at the University of the Philippines-Diliman.
Bagtasa pins the blame on urbanization. While temperatures have broadly spiked across Pagasa stations over the past decade, increases were never the same in many areas.
In the dry months of March-May, for instance, Pagasa data showed significant warming of minimum temperatures in NAIA and Science Garden, while in southern cities like Zamboanga and Daet, temperatures even subsided during in April in the past four years.

Even Baguio City was not spared. While the summer capital of the Philippines still experiences much lower temperatures than the rest of the archipelago, the place is no longer as cold as it was before.
On a yearly basis, Baguio's average minimum temperature rose 1.5 degrees Celsius from 2014 to 2023. Temperatures in the city fall the biggest between late December to February, but over the years, the cooling has subsided.

However, it is difficult to draw conclusions from these numbers, which also do not reflect efficiency in disposing of cases. Case backlogs are an enduring problem in the broader judiciary, and the Supreme Court has made efforts to speed up the resolution of cases, both within its own backyard and in lower courts where it exercises administrative powers.
By tenure, the longest sitting justice to date is Senior Associate Justice Marvic Leonen, who was appointed by the late president Benigno Aquino III, in November 2012. That makes Leonen the ponente of over 800 decisions in court for the past decade, the most among sitting justices, according to court data.
Controlling for the number of months they have occupied their chambers so far, Leonen places third behind Associate Justices Henri Jean Paul Inting and Ramon Paul Hernando on the average number of penned decisions promulgated every month, our analysis showed.
On average, the justices wrote about four decisions a month, data showed.
